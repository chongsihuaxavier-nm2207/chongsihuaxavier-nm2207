Many couples today face great difficulties in purchasing BTO flats, much less resale flats, with sky high prices. Resale flat prices continue to climb for the 31st straight month, as of February 2023, with a 5-room Pinnacle@Duxton Flat sold for $1.34 million. Elsewhere across the island, flats in towns like Bishan and Redhill continue to be extremely popular, attracting tons of interest from prospective buyers.

The Pinnacle@Duxton HDB Estate, which comprises of 7 residential towers of 50 storeys each, is the tallest public housing development in the world

With a complex web of requirements for eligibility, and many factors influencing prices across the island, prospective buyers are understandably confused about the entire process. This datastory hopes to shed some light on this with data sourced from data.gov.sg. You'll be able to understand more about the HDB resale market, like the most significant factors influencing resale prices, and knowing your purchase and subsidy eligibility.

Year of transaction and resale price

The prices of resale flats have been steadily rising since 2019, reaching an average of $549,695 in 2022, from the lowest point of $432,137 in 2019, an increase of over $117,000. During the period of 2017-2022, resale flat prices rose most significantly in 2021, at 12.7%, before slowing to 10.3% in 2022.
